My Experience...

Have a look through the series of events and projects I've been involved with

On-Stage Performance

Joseph and the Technicolour Dreamcoat

Lead Role | SWHS: February 2019

My first time acting, singing and dancing on-stage. The oppurtunity to play this role was quite a surprise to me, having never sang and acted on stage before. I went to the main auditions and quickly got a call back, got asked to audition for the role and was given it! (At this point it's note worthy that the director who cast me is a well established director in the Leicestershire performing arts community). I quickly learnt my lines and songs. The role involved choreographing some scenes myself with fellow cast members. I very much enjoyed playing the part, rehearsing, and helping our brilliant phaoroh learn his lines as he stepped in last minute. We had an audience of approximately 400, 200 per night.

Aladdin Pantomime

Evil Sidekick | Church of Jesus Christ December

I was scouted for this role after being seen Joseph, and I loved every minute. It was a big step up in proffessionalism which was a relief. We had 2 showings in the same day, with a total of 800 audience members. It was the 3rd instance of the churches christmas panto, and as per usual, it was a huge success, among all. The show was also a charity event, and generated a very large amount of canned food donations from audience members.

Off-Stage Performance

FAME! The Musical

Rhythmn Guitarist | SWHS: February 2018

This show was my debut in musical theatre, and despite being a high school show, the band had an impressive quality. I was responsible for managing my own levels and tone, which I did successfully. Desipte being new to playing guitar from sheet music, I learnt very quickly and loved every second of the 4 months of rehearsal and performance.

Sister Act Production

Piano | Wigston College: March 2020

My first time playing piano in a large production, I went from struggling with the first note, to sight- reading the entire 2nd Act. We performed the full show 4 times, with 200+ audience members each night. We were held to a proffessional standard. It was an amazing experience, and the ensemble sounded incredible.

Technical Theatre, Health and Safety, Stage Design, and Stage Setup

Various gear and equipment | SWHS: 2017-2019

I spent hours upon hours working with the set, gear, and tech at SWHS. I rountinely tested the sound and lighting equipment, set up the gear on the staging for almost every show for 3 years (whilst also performing in most of them). when I wasn't setting up or on stage, I was in the tech booth, alternating between doing live sound, and live lighting.
Not a single other student knew that system as well as I did, which benefited me greatly, the gear was always at my disposal, I could get the setup show-ready incredibly quickly, and my extended knowledge of the jester lighting console gave me a wider range of uses that significanly increased performance quality. This, as well as being closely involved with the organisation of the shows earned me the unique contribution to the school award.

Lighting Engineer

Live Lighting | Church of Jesus Christ: December 2018

This was the 2nd iteration of the Churches christmas panto (as mentioned earlier) and its part of how I started in the church panto. It involved the creative use of colour and dynamics to create the best atmosphere possible for the audience. It was a role I treated very seriously. I was also privelleged to work with 2 very experienced and professional sound engineers.

Singing

Leicestershire-based Choir

Tenor | Church of Jesus Christ: 2019-Present Day

After watching this choir perform for years I finally decided it was my turn to join. I started with experience in singing bass, but never tenor. However, through the help of those around me as well as my knowledge of music theory and ear training, I quickly learnt my parts and my ability to sight sing tenor parts is ever increasing.

A Capella

Bass | Wigston College: 2019-2020

After a few years of bass sight-singing experience with Church, I was able to adapt those skills when I joined our colleges A Capella group. So far we've done 2 pentatonix covers, Shallow, and Say Something. These songs have allowed me to develop my sight singing ability, and introduced me to new musical passages, such as arpeggio runs.
